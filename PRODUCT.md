# Product

## Register

product

## Users

12 friends in a private World Cup 2026 sweepstake. Each owns a draw of 4 national teams. Users check in on their phones during matches and throughout the tournament to track standings, live scores, and their teams' progress. Casual sports fans, not analysts — they want the situation at a glance, not a stat deep-dive. Mostly mobile, occasionally desktop.

## Product Purpose

A private sweepstake tracker for a group of friends following the 2026 World Cup. Friends score points as their teams progress through knockouts. The app shows live match scores, the sweepstake leaderboard, team group standings, qualification status, and AI match commentary (John Botson). Success means friends can open the app during half-time and instantly know where they stand.

## Brand Personality

Clean, clear, friendly. Confident without being corporate. Feels like something the group built for themselves — not a product designed by committee. The AI persona (John Botson) keeps a light touch of wit, but the UI stays calm and readable rather than hyped.

## Anti-references

- Casual gamification apps (Duolingo, Wordle clones) — no streaks, badges, or confetti mechanics bleeding into the sweepstake UI. The penalty mini-game is contained; it doesn't set the tone for the whole app.
- Betting sites (Bet365, Paddy Power) — no aggressive color, no odds-style layouts, no garish red/green signaling everywhere.

## Design Principles

1. **Legible under pressure** — scores, ranks, and qualification status must be readable on a glance during a match, not just when focused at a desk.
2. **Friend-group scale** — 12 players, 48 teams, one tournament. Don't over-engineer information density; the data is manageable.
3. **Dark by default** — the app opens in dark mode. Light mode is a toggle, not the primary experience.
4. **One thing per screen** — each tab has a job. Avoid cross-contaminating concerns across views.
5. **Earn every flourish** — motion and decoration should support information, not perform energy.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific WCAG target stated. Minimum bar: body text contrast ≥4.5:1 on both light and dark themes. Live match indicators and qualification badges must be distinguishable without relying solely on color.
