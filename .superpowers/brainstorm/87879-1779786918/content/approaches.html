Penalty Game UI — 3 Redesign Approaches

The core problems: the yellow dot only shows left/right (no height), and the power bar is a horizontal bar labeled "POWER ↑" which doesn't communicate vertical placement. Click an approach to select it.

Recommended
A — 2D Crosshair: cursor shows aim + height simultaneously
The cursor on the goal gains a vertical dimension. Phase 1 sweeps it left/right as before. Phase 2, instead of a separate power bar, the cursor also sweeps up/down — so the crosshair shows the exact landing spot. Label changes: "DIRECTION" → "AIM ←→", "POWER ↑" → "HEIGHT ↕". No conceptual gap between bar and goal.

B — Vertical Height Bar beside the goal
Keep the direction bar as-is. Replace the second horizontal "POWER" bar with a vertical bar on the right side of the goal, visually anchored to the goal height. The cursor on the goal also gains a y-position. The vertical bar makes "where in the goal" intuitively obvious.

C — Visible Goal Zones: bars map to highlighted regions
Divide the goal into a 3×2 grid of named zones (low-left, low-center, low-right, high-left, etc.). The direction bar highlights a column, the height bar highlights a row. The active zone glows on the goal itself. Players see exactly which region they're targeting at all times.
